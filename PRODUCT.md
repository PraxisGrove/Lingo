# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lingo serves readers who want to understand foreign-language webpages without
leaving the original page or losing its context. Its first release is designed
for ordinary readers willing to configure a translation service, BYOK users
who need control over providers, models, cost, and data flow, and organizations
that require approved endpoints and predictable privacy boundaries.

## Product Purpose

Lingo provides continuous bilingual webpage reading with a translation service
chosen by the reader. Success means the original page remains recoverable,
translation state is understandable at every moment, and users can see and
control where page content is sent.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, transparent, and dependable. The interface should feel calm during
reading, precise during configuration, and candid about permissions, failures,
cost, and external data processing.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style composition, decorative product chrome, playful controls
that obscure standard affordances, and interfaces that hide permissions or data
flow. Do not make provider selection or fallback behavior feel automatic when it
requires an explicit user choice.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the reading task primary and make high-frequency page actions immediate.
2. Preserve the original page through every loading, failure, and recovery state.
3. Explain permissions and data destinations at the point where they matter.
4. Prefer familiar, compact controls over decorative presentation.
5. Make every empty or error state lead to a concrete recovery action.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All controls must be keyboard reachable, expose useful names
and state to assistive technology, support light and dark color schemes, honor
reduced-motion preferences, and remain usable in narrow extension popups.
